Jorge Pérez González

Student at University College London

I am a first-year undergraduate MSci Astrophysics student at University College London.

My main scientific interest is in astrobiology. I am very interested in finding potential signs of life in the universe or, at least, finding evidence of planets were life as we know it could potentially develop.

My previous scientific work involves mining transit exoplanet photometry databases like K2 and TESS for the search of variable stars. So far, three of these discoveries have been accepted in the AAVSO International Star Index (VSX) Although not all of them are eclipsing binary stars, my interest comes in the detection and study of these systems since their light curves can be easily confused with those of an exoplanet transit and viceversa. Hence, the necesity of the knowledge of the existance of theses systems to prevent the detection of false positives exoplanet transits.

Besides my scientific interests, In my free time I enjoy photography, skiing and surfing. I also enjoy playing the clarinet and the saxophone.
